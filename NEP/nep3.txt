1.  Question: What is the primary goal of the Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN) mission under NEP 2020?
a) To ensure every child achieves grade-level proficiency in reading, writing, and arithmetic by Grade 5.
b) To provide free textbooks to all students in Grades 1-5.
c) To make all primary schools digitally equipped.
d) To train all primary school teachers in international pedagogy.
Answer: a) To ensure every child achieves grade-level proficiency in reading, writing, and arithmetic by Grade 5.
Explanation: The NEP 2020 identifies the inability to read and write at a basic level as a national crisis. The FLN mission, named "NIPUN Bharat," is a national mission to ensure that by 2025, every child in Grade 3 has foundational learning skills, which is a prerequisite for all further education.
2.  Question: The National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding and Numeracy (NIPUN Bharat) aims to achieve its goals by which year?
a) 2022
b) 2024
c) 2025
d) 2030
Answer: c) 2025
Explanation: NIPUN Bharat is the operational framework for the FLN mission. It has set a clear target of 2025 to ensure that all children in the country attain foundational literacy and numeracy by the end of Grade 3.
3.  Question: According to NEP 2020, the Foundational Stage consists of which grades?
a) Grades 1 to 2
b) Preschool (age 3) to Grade 2
c) Preschool (age 3) to Grade 3
d) Grades 1 to 5
Answer: b) Preschool (age 3) to Grade 2
Explanation: NEP 2020 restructures the school education into a 5+3+3+4 design. The first stage is the Foundational Stage, which covers 5 years of early childhood care and education (ECCE) for children aged 3-8, including three years of preschool/Anganwadi and Grades 1 and 2.
4.  Question: What pedagogical approach is emphasized for the Foundational Stage?
a) Rote learning and memorization
b) Play-based, activity-based, and inquiry-based learning
c) Lecture-based and textbook-centric learning
d) Standardized testing and examinations
Answer: b) Play-based, activity-based, and inquiry-based learning
Explanation: For young children in the Foundational Stage, NEP 2020 strongly advocates for a pedagogy that is developmentally appropriate. This includes learning through play, discovery, and activity to make learning joyful and effective.
5.  Question: What is the role of a teacher in the FLN mission as per NEP 2020?
a) To be a strict disciplinarian
b) To be a facilitator of learning, providing individualized support
c) To focus only on completing the textbook syllabus
d) To prepare students for competitive exams from an early age
Answer: b) To be a facilitator of learning, providing individualized support
Explanation: The policy envisions a shift in the teacher's role from a mere instructor to a facilitator. In the context of FLN, this means understanding the diverse learning levels of students and providing personalized support to help them achieve foundational skills.
6.  Question: The term 'Universal Acquisition of Foundational Literacy and Numeracy' in NEP 2020 refers to achieving this goal by which grade?
a) Grade 1
b) Grade 2
c) Grade 3
d) Grade 5
Answer: c) Grade 3
Explanation: The policy states that the highest priority of the education system will be to achieve universal foundational literacy and numeracy in primary school by 2025. This is defined as every child being able to read with comprehension and perform basic math by the end of Grade 3.
7.  Question: What is the primary mechanism proposed by NEP 2020 to track the progress of FLN?
a) National Achievement Test (NAS)
b) A national repository of high-quality resources for Foundational Literacy and Numeracy
c) School-based assessment with a focus on continuous and comprehensive evaluation
d) A single, high-stakes board exam at the end of Grade 2
Answer: c) School-based assessment with a focus on continuous and comprehensive evaluation
Explanation: NEP 2020 emphasizes moving away from high-stakes exams. For FLN, it recommends robust school-based assessment systems that are diagnostic and provide feedback to teachers and students to improve learning, rather than just ranking them.
8.  Question: Who is responsible for implementing the FLN mission at the ground level?
a) Central Government only
b) State Governments only
c) A joint effort between the Centre, States, and local bodies
d) Private schools only
Answer: c) A joint effort between the Centre, States, and local bodies
Explanation: The policy stresses a collaborative, whole-of-society approach. The central government provides the national mission framework and funding, while state governments and local bodies (like schools and panchayats) are responsible for its actual implementation and contextualization.
9.  Question: Which of the following is NOT a component of the FLN mission?
a) Teacher training
b) Providing a national textbook for all Grade 1 students
c) Developing learning materials in the mother tongue
d) Parental and community involvement
Answer: b) Providing a national textbook for all Grade 1 students
Explanation: NEP 2020 advocates for local context and flexibility. While a national curriculum framework (NCF) will guide the process, a single, one-size-fits-all textbook is not the approach. Instead, high-quality, flexible learning materials are encouraged.
10. Question: The FLN mission will be overseen by which body?
a) National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT)
b) A National Mission on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy
c) Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE)
d) University Grants Commission (UGC)
Answer: b) A National Mission on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy
Explanation: The policy explicitly calls for the creation of a National Mission on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy under the Ministry of Education to oversee, guide, and fund the implementation of this critical mission.
--- Section 2: School Education Structure and Curriculum ---
11. Question: What is the new school education structure proposed by NEP 2020?
a) 8+2+2
b) 10+2
c) 5+3+3+4
d) 4+4+4
Answer: c) 5+3+3+4
Explanation: NEP 2020 replaces the decades-old 10+2 structure with a new curricular and pedagogical structure of 5+3+3+4, covering ages 3-18. This is designed to bring Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) within the formal education framework.
12. Question: The '5' in the 5+3+3+4 structure corresponds to which stage?
a) Preparatory Stage
b) Middle Stage
c) Foundational Stage
d) Secondary Stage
Answer: c) Foundational Stage
Explanation: The first 5 years (ages 3-8) constitute the Foundational Stage, which includes 3 years of pre-primary education (Anganwadi/preschool) and Grades 1 and 2.
13. Question: The 'Preparatory Stage' in the new structure covers which grades?
a) Grades 1 to 2
b) Grades 3 to 5
c) Grades 6 to 8
d) Grades 9 to 10
Answer: b) Grades 3 to 5
Explanation: The Preparatory Stage (ages 8-11, Grades 3-5) builds upon the foundational stage. The focus here is on developing language and numeracy skills through interactive and classroom-based learning, while also introducing subjects like science and social studies.
14. Question: The 'Middle Stage' in the new structure corresponds to which grades?
a) Grades 3 to 5
b) Grades 6 to 8
c) Grades 9 to 10
d) Grades 11 to 12
Answer: b) Grades 6 to 8
Explanation: The Middle Stage (ages 11-14, Grades 6-8) introduces subject teachers and more abstract concepts in sciences, mathematics, arts, social sciences, and humanities. The pedagogy moves towards experiential learning in the sciences and mathematics.
15. Question: The 'Secondary Stage' in the new structure covers which grades?
a) Grades 6 to 8
b) Grades 9 to 10
c) Grades 9 to 12
d) Grades 11 to 12
Answer: c) Grades 9 to 12
Explanation: The Secondary Stage (ages 14-18, Grades 9-12) is a four-year stage. It is multidisciplinary, with students having greater choice and flexibility in selecting subjects. It is divided into two phases: Grades 9-10 and Grades 11-12.
16. Question: What is the primary aim of the curriculum and pedagogy in the new structure?
a) To reduce the burden of textbooks
b) To develop the cognitive, social, and emotional capacities of a student
c) To focus solely on academic excellence
d) To align with international curriculum standards
Answer: b) To develop the cognitive, social, and emotional capacities of a student
Explanation: The overarching goal of the new curriculum and pedagogy is the holistic development of the child. It aims to move away from rote learning towards developing critical thinking, creativity, and socio-emotional skills.
17. Question: What is the 'Curricular Integration of Essential Subjects and Skills' in NEP 2020?
a) Merging all subjects into one combined subject
b) Integrating subjects like arts, sports, and vocational skills with academic subjects
c) Focusing only on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)
d) Making all subjects optional
Answer: b) Integrating subjects like arts, sports, and vocational skills with academic subjects
Explanation: The policy emphasizes that subjects like arts, music, sports, and vocational crafts are not extracurricular but are essential parts of the curriculum. They should be integrated to provide a well-rounded and enjoyable education.
18. Question: What is the recommended medium of instruction up to at least Grade 5?
a) English
b) Hindi
c) Mother tongue / local language
d) Sanskrit
Answer: c) Mother tongue / local language
Explanation: NEP 2020 strongly advocates that the mother tongue or local language should be the medium of instruction at least until Grade 5, and preferably till Grade 8 and beyond. This is based on research showing that children learn foundational concepts most effectively in their home language.
19. Question: The 'three languages learned by children will be the choices of States, regions, and of course the students themselves' is a key principle of NEP 2020's policy on:
a) Vocational Education
b) Digital Education
c) Languages
d) Teacher Education
Answer: c) Languages
Explanation: The policy reiterates the three-language formula but provides greater flexibility. The states, regions, and students will have the freedom to choose the languages they want to learn, as long as at least two of the three are native to India.
20. Question: What is the significance of 'bagless days' in the school curriculum as per NEP 2020?
a) To give students holidays from school
b) To engage students in arts, sports, and vocational activities
c) To reduce the weight of school bags
d) To allow teachers to plan their lessons
Answer: b) To engage students in arts, sports, and vocational activities
Explanation: The policy suggests that every school should organize a few 'bagless days' each year, where students are freed from regular classes to engage in enriching activities like art, craft, sports, and vocational training, fostering creativity and practical skills.
21. Question: The new school structure aims to bring which of the following under the formal education ambit?
a) Higher Education
b) Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE)
c) Vocational Education
d) Teacher Training
Answer: b) Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE)
Explanation: A major structural change is the inclusion of the 3 years of pre-schooling/Anganwadi (ECCE) within the formal school structure as part of the 5-year Foundational Stage. This recognizes the critical importance of early learning.
22. Question: The NEP 2020 aims to reduce the curriculum content to enhance:
a) Exam scores
b) Essential learning and critical thinking
c) Teacher workload
d) School hours
Answer: b) Essential learning and critical thinking
Explanation: The policy calls for a reduction in the curriculum load to its core essentials. The goal is to make space for more in-depth understanding, critical thinking, analysis, and application-based learning, moving away from rote memorization of vast content.
23. Question: What is the purpose of introducing 'conceptual understanding' in the curriculum?
a) To make the curriculum easier
b) To ensure students can apply knowledge to real-world situations
c) To focus only on theoretical knowledge
d) To prepare students for specific jobs
Answer: b) To ensure students can apply knowledge to real-world situations
Explanation: The emphasis on conceptual understanding means that students should not just know facts but understand the underlying principles and be able to apply this knowledge to solve problems and understand the world around them.
24. Question: The policy envisions a shift from content-based learning to:
a) Rote-based learning
b) Competency-based learning
c) Textbook-based learning
d) Teacher-centric learning
Answer: b) Competency-based learning
Explanation: A key pedagogical shift is towards competency-based learning and education. This means the focus is on what students can do with the knowledge they acquire, developing specific skills and competencies at each stage.
25. Question: What is the role of the National Curriculum Framework (NCF) in the new curriculum?
a) To prescribe a single textbook for the entire country
b) To provide a framework for developing curriculum, syllabi, and teaching materials
c) To conduct national-level exams
d) To recruit teachers
Answer: b) To provide a framework for developing curriculum, syllabi, and teaching materials
Explanation: The NCF, developed by NCERT, serves as a guiding document. It outlines the vision, principles, and structure of the curriculum. States and boards can then use this framework to develop their own syllabi and materials that are contextually relevant.
--- Section 3: Assessment Reforms ---
26. Question: NEP 2020 proposes to replace the existing system of summative assessment with which type of assessment?
a) Formative and competency-based assessment
b) Annual high-stakes examination
c) Peer assessment only
d) Parent-led assessment
Answer: a) Formative and competency-based assessment
Explanation: The policy aims to shift the focus from assessment of learning to assessment for learning. This means using regular, formative assessments to provide feedback and improve the learning process, rather than just for summative grading at the end of the year.
27. Question: According to NEP 2020, the main purpose of assessment should be:
a) To rank students
b) To promote learning and development
c) To certify students for the next grade
d) To evaluate teacher performance
Answer: b) To promote learning and development
Explanation: The core philosophy of assessment reform is to make it a tool for continuous improvement. It should help students identify their strengths and weaknesses and help teachers adjust their pedagogy to support student learning.
28. Question: The 'PARAKH' (Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development) is a proposed:
a) New national board of examination
b) National assessment centre
c) Teacher training institute
d) University for research in education
Answer: b) National assessment centre
Explanation: NEP 2020 proposes setting up a National Assessment Centre, PARAKH, as a standard-setting body for student assessment. It will develop norms, standards, and guidelines for student assessment and evaluation across all school boards.
29. Question: What is the proposed change for board examinations (e.g., CBSE Class 10 and 12)?
a) They will be made more difficult to increase standards.
b) They will be conducted twice a year.
c) They will be abolished completely.
d) They will be conducted only for students who wish to appear.
Answer: b) They will be conducted twice a year.
Explanation: The policy suggests that board exams will be redesigned to be less high-stakes. They will be offered twice a year to give students more than one opportunity to perform well. The exams will also focus on testing core competencies rather than memorized facts.
30. Question: What is the proposed structure for the new board examinations?
a) A single, final exam covering the entire syllabus.
b) Two parts: an objective section and a subjective section.
c) An objective-type test with a focus on application-based questions.
d) A practical examination with no written test.
Answer: c) An objective-type test with a focus on application-based questions.
Explanation: The redesigned board exams will aim to test conceptual understanding and the ability to apply knowledge. They will have a significant proportion of objective-type questions (like multiple choice) and questions that assess higher-order thinking skills.
31. Question: The NEP 2020 states that students will be allowed to choose:
a) Their teachers
b) The subjects in which they want to be tested in board exams
c) The difficulty level of their exams
d) The school they want to take their exam in
Answer: b) The subjects in which they want to be tested in board exams
Explanation: To reduce pressure and allow students to pursue their interests, the policy suggests that students may have the option to choose the subjects in which they want to take board examinations, in addition to the compulsory subjects.
32. Question: The 'Holistic Progress Card' is a tool for:
a) Tracking a student's academic scores only.
b) A 360-degree, multidimensional report of a student's progress.
c) Ranking schools based on student performance.
d) Providing feedback to teachers only.
Answer: b) A 360-degree, multidimensional report of a student's progress.
Explanation: The policy proposes replacing the traditional report card with a Holistic Progress Card. This card will be a comprehensive record of a student's development, including not only academics but also skills, talents, and socio-emotional well-being, with inputs from teachers, peers, and the student themselves.
33. Question: What is the proposed role of AI in school assessment as per NEP 2020?
a) To replace teachers in grading
b) To develop personalized learning pathways and track student progress
c) To create question papers for all exams
d) To conduct online proctoring for all tests
Answer: b) To develop personalized learning pathways and track student progress
Explanation: The policy encourages the use of technology like AI to create personalized adaptive learning systems. These systems can track a student's learning progress in real-time and provide customized content and feedback to help them learn at their own pace.
34. Question: For Grades 3, 5, and 8, NEP 2020 proposes:
a) High-stakes board examinations.
b) No formal assessment, only observation.
c) Regular, school-based assessment to track learning progress.
d) A single national-level examination.
Answer: c) Regular, school-based assessment to track learning progress.
Explanation: The policy explicitly states that there will be no board examinations for these grades. Instead, a robust system of school-based assessment will be used to monitor progress and ensure that learning outcomes are being met, particularly for Foundational Literacy and Numeracy.
35. Question: The ultimate goal of assessment reforms in NEP 2020 is to:
a) Make Indian students globally competitive in exams.
b) Reduce the stress and anxiety associated with exams.
c) Ensure all students get high marks.
d) Increase the number of students going to college.
Answer: b) Reduce the stress and anxiety associated with exams.
Explanation: A key driver of the assessment reforms is the recognition that the current high-stakes exam system causes immense stress and anxiety among students. The changes are designed to make assessment a more supportive and developmental process.
36. Question: The new assessment system will focus on:
a) Knowledge and recall of facts.
b) Analysis, critical thinking, and conceptual clarity.
c) Handwriting and presentation skills.
d) Speed of completing the exam.
Answer: b) Analysis, critical thinking, and conceptual clarity.
Explanation: The shift is from rote learning to assessing higher-order thinking skills. Questions will be designed to test a student's ability to analyze information, think critically, solve problems, and demonstrate a clear understanding of concepts.
37. Question: The 'Self-Assessment' component in the Holistic Progress Card is meant to:
a) Allow students to grade themselves.
b) Encourage students to reflect on their own learning and progress.
c) Replace teacher assessment completely.
d) Be used as the primary criteria for promotion.
Answer: b) Encourage students to reflect on their own learning and progress.
Explanation: Self-assessment is a key part of making students active participants in their own education. It encourages metacognition (thinking about one's own thinking) and helps students become more aware of their strengths and areas for improvement.
38. Question: The NEP 2020's assessment reforms are aligned with its goal of:
a) Increasing the number of private schools.
b) Moving towards a more holistic and multidisciplinary education.
c) Standardizing the curriculum across all states.
d) Promoting English as the sole medium of instruction.
Answer: b) Moving towards a more holistic and multidisciplinary education.
Explanation: The assessment reforms are not an isolated change. They are intrinsically linked to the broader vision of creating a more holistic, flexible, and student-centric education system that values diverse talents and skills.
39. Question: The National Assessment Centre, PARAKH, will be set up under which existing body?
a) NCERT
b) CBSE
c) UGC
d) A new autonomous body under the Ministry of Education
Answer: a) NCERT
Explanation: The policy states that PARAKH will be set up as a constituent unit of NCERT. This will allow it to work closely with curriculum developers and educational researchers to create a coherent system of standards and assessments.
40. Question: The '360-degree' progress report includes feedback from:
a) Teachers and parents only.
b) Teachers and students only.
c) Teachers, students, and peers.
d) Teachers, students, peers, and community members.
Answer: c) Teachers, students, and peers.
Explanation: The Holistic Progress Card is designed to be a comprehensive tool. It includes self-assessment by the student, assessment by the teacher, and peer assessment, providing a more rounded and accurate picture of the student's development.
--- Section 4: Teacher Education and Development ---
41. Question: By what year does NEP 2020 aim to ensure that all teachers at all levels are trained?
a) 2022
b) 2025
c) 2030
d) 2035
Answer: c) 2030
Explanation: The policy sets a target that by 2030, the minimum degree qualification for teaching will be a 4-year integrated B.Ed. degree. This implies a phased transition and training for the existing teacher workforce to meet this new standard.
42. Question: What is the new minimum qualification proposed for a teacher by 2030?
a) 2-year B.Ed. after Bachelor's degree
b) 4-year integrated B.Ed. degree
c) D.El.Ed (Diploma in Elementary Education)
d) Any Bachelor's degree with a teaching certificate
Answer: b) 4-year integrated B.Ed. degree
Explanation: To improve the quality of teacher education, NEP 2020 mandates a move towards a 4-year integrated B.Ed. program that combines a Bachelor's degree in a subject with a B.Ed. This will create a more seamless and high-quality preparation for teachers.
43. Question: The policy proposes to replace the existing B.Ed. and D.El.Ed programs with:
a) A single 2-year teacher training program
b) A 4-year integrated teacher education program
c) An online teacher training module
d) No formal teacher training program
Answer: b) A 4-year integrated teacher education program
Explanation: The goal is to phase out the fragmented system of teacher education (B.Ed. for secondary, D.El.Ed. for elementary) and move towards a unified, high-quality 4-year integrated B.Ed. program for all levels of school teaching.
44. Question: What is the proposed mechanism for the continuous professional development (CPD) of teachers?
a) Mandatory refresher courses every 5 years
b) 50 hours of CPD per year
c) Linking promotion to CPD completion
d) A one-time training at the beginning of their career
Answer: b) 50 hours of CPD per year
Explanation: The policy mandates that teachers will be required to undertake 50 hours of Continuous Professional Development (CPD) every year. This will empower them to update their knowledge and skills and stay abreast of new pedagogical practices.
45. Question: What is the proposed name for the new autonomous bodies for teacher education?
a) District Institutes of Education and Training (DIETs)
b) State Councils of Educational Research and Training (SCERTs)
c) National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE)
d) None of the above
Answer: d) None of the above
Explanation: The policy proposes to restructure and re-energize existing bodies like DIETs and SCERTs. It also calls for the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) to function more as a professional standard-setting body, but it doesn't propose a new name for these institutions.
46. Question: The policy suggests that teacher recruitment and deployment should be based on:
a) Seniority
b) A transparent online system with school-specific vacancies
c) Political recommendations
d) A centralized national-level test only
Answer: b) A transparent online system with school-specific vacancies
Explanation: To ensure transparency and efficiency, NEP 2020 advocates for a technology-based, transparent system for teacher recruitment and transfers. This system would post school-specific vacancies, allowing teachers to apply directly to the schools where they are needed.
47. Question: What is the proposed role of the National Professional Standards for Teachers (NPST)?
a) To set the minimum qualifications for teachers
b) To define the competencies and standards for teachers at different stages
c) To conduct the B.Ed. examinations
d) To certify teachers for promotion
Answer: b) To define the competencies and standards for teachers at different stages
Explanation: The NPST will be a framework that outlines what a teacher should know and be able to do. It will cover various aspects of teaching, including ethics, pedagogy, knowledge of the subject, and student engagement. This will guide teacher education, recruitment, and CPD.
48. Question: What is the vision for the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) under NEP 2020?
a) To be a body that approves teacher education institutes
b) To function as a professional standard-setting body under a General Education Council
c) To conduct the CTET exam
d) To be merged with the UGC
Answer: b) To function as a professional standard-setting body under a General Education Council
Explanation: The policy proposes to reconstitute the NCTE. Its primary role will shift from being an 'approving' body to a professional body that sets standards for curriculum, pedagogy, assessment, and professional development for teachers.
49. Question: The policy aims to give teachers more:
a) Administrative work
b) Autonomy in choosing pedagogy and assessment methods
c) Non-teaching duties like election work
d) Paperwork and reporting requirements
Answer: b) Autonomy in choosing pedagogy and assessment methods
Explanation: Recognizing teachers as professionals, NEP 2020 advocates for giving them greater autonomy and agency. This includes the freedom to choose the best pedagogical methods and assessment tools for their students, within the framework of the curriculum.
50. Question: To attract high-quality individuals to the teaching profession, the policy suggests:
a) Increasing the salary of teachers
b) Providing scholarships for the 4-year integrated B.Ed. program
c) Making the profession more respectable and empowering
d) All of the above
Answer: d) All of the above
Explanation: The policy acknowledges the need to make the teaching profession more attractive. It suggests a multi-pronged approach including better service conditions, career progression, professional respect, and scholarships for meritorious students entering teacher education programs.
51. Question: The 'teacher-eligibility test' will be restructured to:
a) Be conducted only once a year
b) Test only subject knowledge
c) Test a wider range of abilities, including child development and pedagogy
d) Be made optional for private schools
Answer: c) Test a wider range of abilities, including child development and pedagogy
Explanation: The policy calls for the TET (like CTET) to be strengthened and restructured. It should go beyond just testing content knowledge and also assess a candidate's understanding of child psychology, pedagogy, and classroom management skills.
52. Question: The policy emphasizes that the professional development of teachers should be:
a) One-size-fits-all
b) Mandated and top-down
c) Need-based, personalized, and teacher-driven
d) Focused only on digital literacy
Answer: c) Need-based, personalized, and teacher-driven
Explanation: The vision for CPD is that it should not be a generic, top-down process. Instead, it should be tailored to the individual needs of teachers, allowing them to choose areas for their own growth and development.
53. Question: What is the proposed role of 'Mentor Teachers' or 'Master Teachers'?
a) To inspect schools
b) To conduct administrative duties
c) To mentor and guide new teachers and teachers who need support
d) To write textbooks
Answer: c) To mentor and guide new teachers and teachers who need support
Explanation: The policy suggests creating a system of mentorship where experienced and skilled teachers can act as mentors. They will provide on-the-job guidance, support, and professional development to other teachers, especially those in the early stages of their careers.
54. Question: The NEP 2020's focus on teacher empowerment is intended to lead to:
a) A decrease in the workload of teachers
b) An improvement in student learning outcomes
c) An increase in the number of private schools
d) A reduction in the education budget
Answer: b) An improvement in student learning outcomes
Explanation: The ultimate goal of empowering and developing teachers is to improve the quality of teaching and learning in the classroom. Better-trained, motivated, and autonomous teachers are the key to enhancing student learning outcomes.
55. Question: The policy states that teachers should be given:
a) More non-academic responsibilities
b) Opportunities for career progression based on merit and performance
c) A fixed curriculum that they cannot change
d) Less say in school management
Answer: b) Opportunities for career progression based on merit and performance
Explanation: To make the teaching profession a viable and attractive career path, NEP 2020 calls for clear pathways for career progression. This should be based on merit, performance, and professional development, rather than just seniority.
56. Question: The 'School Complexes' concept will help in teacher development by:
a) Reducing the number of teachers required
b) Creating a cluster of schools for sharing resources and collaborative teacher training
c) Centralizing all teacher training in one location
d) Separating primary and secondary teachers
Answer: b) Creating a cluster of schools for sharing resources and collaborative teacher training
Explanation: The School Complex is a key governance and resource-sharing mechanism. A group of schools (including secondary and primary) in a geographic area will form a complex. This allows for sharing of teachers, labs, libraries, and for conducting joint professional development workshops and peer learning.
57. Question: The NEP 2020's approach to teacher education is based on the principle of:
a) Centralization
b) Standardization
c) Professionalization
d) Privatization
Answer: c) Professionalization
Explanation: The entire set of reforms for teacher education, from the 4-year B.Ed. to CPD and NPST, is aimed at elevating the status of teaching to a true profession, with high standards for entry, continuous development, and ethical practice.
58. Question: The policy aims to ensure that teacher education institutions:
a) Focus only on theoretical knowledge
b) Become vibrant, multidisciplinary institutions
c) Are run only by the government
d) Reduce their intake of students
Answer: b) Become vibrant, multidisciplinary institutions
Explanation: NEP 2020 wants teacher education colleges to move away from being isolated, single-discipline institutions. It encourages them to become multidisciplinary, offering programs in arts, sciences, and vocational subjects alongside teacher education, to create a richer academic environment.
59. Question: The policy proposes to strengthen the DIETs to:
a) Function as centers for academic support and teacher development at the district level
b) Become primary schools
c) Conduct board examinations
d) Manage school finances
Answer: a) Function as centers for academic support and teacher development at the district level
Explanation: District Institutes of Education and Training (DIETs) will be reinvigorated to become the hub for all academic and resource support for schools and teachers in a district. They will lead curriculum development, teacher training, and assessment initiatives at the local level.
60. Question: The ultimate goal of all teacher education reforms in NEP 2020 is:
a) To reduce the cost of teacher education
b) To create a new cadre of highly qualified, motivated, and empowered teachers
c) To make teaching a part-time profession
d) To align teacher education with foreign models
Answer: b) To create a new cadre of highly qualified, motivated, and empowered teachers
Explanation: All the changes—from the 4-year B.Ed. to CPD and NPST—are aimed at fundamentally transforming the teaching profession. The vision is to have teachers who are not just well-qualified but are also passionate, empowered, and capable of nurturing the potential of every student.
--- Section 5: Higher Education ---
61. Question: What is the proposed new structure for higher education degrees (UG/PG)?
a) 3-year UG and 2-year PG
b) 4-year UG with multiple exit options and 1-year or 2-year PG
c) 2-year UG and 3-year PG
d) A single 5-year integrated degree
Answer: b) 4-year UG with multiple exit options and 1-year or 2-year PG
Explanation: NEP 2020 overhauls the UG structure. It introduces a 4-year multidisciplinary UG program with flexible exit options: a certificate after 1 year, a diploma after 2 years, a Bachelor's degree after 3 years, and a Bachelor's with Research after 4 years. The PG program will be 1 or 2 years.
62. Question: The 'multiple entry and exit' options in higher education are meant to provide:
a) An easy way for students to drop out
b) Flexibility and a credit-based system for students
c) A reduction in the total duration of a degree
d) A standardized curriculum for all universities
Answer: b) Flexibility and a credit-based system for students
Explanation: This system is designed to give students flexibility in their learning journey. They can take a break from their studies and rejoin later, with the credits they have earned being saved in an Academic Bank of Credit (ABC). This allows for a more personalized and fluid educational path.
63. Question: What is the 'Academic Bank of Credit' (ABC)?
a) A bank that provides loans to students
b) A digital repository of credits earned by students
c) A ranking agency for universities
d) A scholarship fund
Answer: b) A digital repository of credits earned by students
Explanation: The ABC will be a digital platform that will formally store the credits earned by a student from various higher education institutions. These credits can be verified, accumulated, and transferred, allowing students to re-enter the education system at a later stage.
64. Question: The policy aims to increase the Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER) in higher education to what percentage by 2035?
a) 25%
b) 30%
c) 50%
d) 75%
Answer: c) 50%
Explanation: The policy sets an ambitious target of increasing the Gross Enrolment Ratio in higher education from the current ~26% to 50% by 2035. This involves increasing access, capacity, and quality in the higher education system.
65. Question: What is the proposed governing body for higher education, replacing bodies like UGC and AICTE?
a) Higher Education Commission of India (HECI)
b) National Education Commission
c) All India Council for Higher Education (AICHE)
d) University Grants Commission (UGC)
Answer: a) Higher Education Commission of India (HECI)
Explanation: NEP 2020 proposes to replace the existing complex regulatory architecture (UGC, AICTE, etc.) with a single, umbrella body called the Higher Education Commission of India (HECI). HECI will have four independent verticals for regulation, accreditation, funding, and academic standard-setting.
66. Question: Which of the following is NOT one of the four verticals of HECI?
a) National Higher Education Regulatory Council (NHERC)
b) National Accreditation Council (NAC)
c) Higher Education Grants Council (HEGC)
d) National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE)
Answer: d) National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE)
Explanation: The four verticals of HECI are: NHERC (for regulation), NAC (for accreditation), HEGC (for funding), and the General Education Council (for setting academic standards, including the National Higher Education Qualification Framework). NCTE is proposed to be restructured under the school education framework.
67. Question: The policy promotes 'multidisciplinary education' in higher education, which means:
a) Students must study only one subject in depth.
b) Students have the flexibility to choose subjects from different disciplines.
c) All universities will offer the same set of courses.
d) Focus will be only on professional and technical courses.
Answer: b) Students have the flexibility to choose subjects from different disciplines.
Explanation: The vision is to move away from rigid, siloed streams (Arts, Science, Commerce). Multidisciplinary education allows students to design their own curriculum by combining subjects from different fields, such as a science student studying a language or an arts student studying a basic science course.
68. Question: What is the target for public investment in education as a percentage of GDP?
a) 4%
b) 6%
c) 8%
d) 10%
Answer: b) 6%
Explanation: The policy reiterates the long-standing goal of increasing public investment in education to reach 6% of the country's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at the earliest. This is crucial for funding the various ambitious initiatives outlined in the policy.
69. Question: What is the proposed status for college affiliation?
a) All colleges must be affiliated to a university.
b) The system of affiliation will be phased out over 15 years, and colleges will become autonomous degree-granting institutions.
c) Only private colleges will be affiliated.
d) Affiliation will be made stricter with more regulations.
Answer: b) The system of affiliation will be phased out over 15 years, and colleges will become autonomous degree-granting institutions.
Explanation: The policy recognizes the problems with the large, unwieldy affiliated college system. It proposes a gradual phasing out of this system, with colleges developing into autonomous, multidisciplinary institutions that can eventually grant their own degrees.
70. Question: The NEP 2020 proposes to establish which type of universities to achieve global standards?
a) Private universities
b) Central universities
c) Multidisciplinary Education and Research Universities (MERUs)
d) State universities
Answer: c) Multidisciplinary Education and Research Universities (MERUs)
Explanation: To create world-class institutions, the policy proposes setting up MERUs. These will be large, multidisciplinary universities focusing on both high-quality teaching and cutting-edge research, aiming to compete with the best universities globally.
71. Question: What is the purpose of the National Research Foundation (NRF)?
a) To fund only science and technology research
b) To fund, coordinate, and nurture research culture across all disciplines
c) To regulate research in universities
d) To provide scholarships for PhD students
Answer: b) To fund, coordinate, and nurture research culture across all disciplines
Explanation: The NRF is a crucial proposal to significantly boost India's research and innovation ecosystem. It will be an autonomous body that will provide competitive funding for research projects across all fields, including social sciences, arts, and humanities, and not just STEM.
72. Question: The policy aims to make higher education:
a) More centralized and uniform
b) More accessible, equitable, and inclusive
c) Focused only on STEM fields
d) Privately funded
Answer: b) More accessible, equitable, and inclusive
Explanation: A core principle of NEP 2020 is inclusion. The policy has several measures to increase access to higher education for students from disadvantaged backgrounds, including scholarships, support systems, and creating more opportunities in underserved regions.
73. Question: The 4-year UG program with a research component is aimed at:
a) Preparing students for PhD programs directly
b) Reducing the duration of a Master's degree
c) Making the UG program more difficult
d) Discouraging students from pursuing higher studies
Answer: a) Preparing students for PhD programs directly
Explanation: The 4th year of the UG program will have a strong research component. Students who complete this 4-year program will be eligible to directly enter a PhD program, bypassing the need for a Master's degree in many cases. This streamlines the path to a research career.
74. Question: The policy proposes to transform the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) into:
a) Purely research institutes
b) Multidisciplinary institutions
c) Universities focused only on humanities
d) Vocational training centers
Answer: b) Multidisciplinary institutions
Explanation: While maintaining their core strengths, the policy encourages premier institutions like IITs and IIMs to become more holistic by adding programs in humanities, social sciences, arts, and management, thus becoming truly multidisciplinary.
75. Question: The 'National Institutional Ranking Framework' (NIRF) will be used to:
a) Provide more funding to top-ranked institutes only
b) Provide a transparent ranking system to help students make informed choices
c) Shut down low-ranked institutes
d) Standardize the curriculum across all institutes
Answer: b) Provide a transparent ranking system to help students make informed choices
Explanation: NIRF is an existing framework that will be strengthened. Its purpose is to provide a credible, transparent, and objective ranking of higher education institutions based on various parameters. This helps students and parents make informed decisions and also encourages institutions to improve their performance.
76. Question: The policy's vision for higher education is rooted in:
a) Indian ethos and values
b) Western models of education
c) A purely economic and utilitarian view of education
d) A focus on rote learning and examinations
Answer: a) Indian ethos and values
Explanation: The policy emphasizes that higher education should be deeply rooted in Indian culture and ethos. It aims to develop individuals who are not only globally competent but also have a strong sense of social responsibility and an appreciation for India's rich heritage and knowledge systems.
77. Question: The NEP 2020 aims to internationalize education in India by:
a) Making English the compulsory medium of instruction
b) Allowing top foreign universities to set up campuses in India
c) Sending all Indian students to study abroad
d) Banning foreign degrees in India
Answer: b) Allowing top foreign universities to set up campuses in India
Explanation: To promote internationalization and provide global opportunities to Indian students, the policy proposes a regulatory facilitation mechanism to allow the top 100 universities in the world to operate in India.
78. Question: What is the role of the 'General Education Council' (GEC)?
a) To regulate higher education institutions
b) To set academic standards, including the National Higher Education Qualification Framework (NHEQF)
c) To provide funding to universities
d) To accredit colleges
Answer: b) To set academic standards, including the National Higher Education Qualification Framework (NHEQF)
Explanation: The GEC is one of the four verticals of the proposed HECI. Its primary function is to be the apex body for setting academic standards, defining learning outcomes, and creating the NHEQF, which will specify the qualifications for each level of higher education.
79. Question: The policy emphasizes creating 'institutions of eminence' to:
a) Reduce the number of universities in India

d) Provide education only to foreign students
Answer: b) Build world-class universities that can compete globally
Explanation: The 'Institutions of Eminence' (IoE) scheme is a key initiative to identify and empower 20 institutions (10 public and 10 private) with greater autonomy and funding to help them achieve world-class status and rank among the top global universities.
80. Question: The ultimate goal of higher education reforms in NEP 2020 is to create a higher education system that is:
a) Centralized and rigid
b) High-quality, equitable, and vibrant
c) Focused only on job placement
d) Accessible only to the elite
Answer: b) High-quality, equitable, and vibrant
Explanation: The overarching vision is to transform the higher education system into one that is not only large and accessible but also of high quality, fostering critical thinking, research, innovation, and holistic development.
--- Section 6: Vocational Education and Skill Development ---
81. Question: By what year does NEP 2020 aim to integrate vocational education into all schools and higher education institutions?
a) 2025
b) 2030
c) 2035
d) 2040
Answer: a) 2025
Explanation: The policy sets a target to integrate vocational education into the educational ecosystem in a phased manner. By 2025, at least 50% of learners in schools and higher education will have had exposure to vocational education.
82. Question: Vocational education will be integrated as a subject in schools from which grade?
a) Grade 1
b) Grade 3
c) Grade 6
d) Grade 9
Answer: c) Grade 6
Explanation: The policy proposes introducing vocational education as an integral part of the curriculum from Grade 6 onwards. This is a significant shift from treating it as a separate, optional stream.
83. Question: What is the proposed model for vocational education in schools?
a) Separate vocational schools
b) A 'hub and spoke' model with schools acting as spokes and local industry/institutes as hubs
c) Online-only vocational courses
d) Making it a mandatory subject for board exams
Answer: b) A 'hub and spoke' model with schools acting as spokes and local industry/institutes as hubs
Explanation: To provide practical, hands-on training, the policy suggests a 'hub and spoke' arrangement. Schools (spokes) will be linked with local vocational skill providers, polytechnics, or industries (hubs) where students can get practical experience.
84. Question: According to NEP 2020, vocational education should be treated as:
a) An inferior alternative to academic education
b) An integral part of education, on par with academic subjects
c) An option only for low-performing students
d) A separate stream after Grade 10
Answer: b) An integral part of education, on par with academic subjects
Explanation: A key objective is to remove the social stigma associated with vocational education. The policy emphasizes that it should be a dignified and respectable choice, integrated with mainstream education and not seen as a track for underperforming students.
85. Question: The '10 days of internship' for students from Grade 6 to 12 is intended to:
a) Give students a break from studies
b) Provide them with hands-on experience and exposure to different vocations
c) Help schools earn extra money
d) Fulfill a requirement for board exams
Answer: b) Provide them with hands-on experience and exposure to different vocations
Explanation: To make vocational learning practical, the policy mandates that every student will undertake a fun, exploratory, and hands-on internship for 10 days each year. This will expose them to various vocations and help them understand the world of work.
86. Question: The policy envisions 'lifelong learning' through:
a) Formal degree programs only
b) Multiple entry and exit options and a credit accumulation system
c) A single, final examination
d) Restricting education to a certain age group
Answer: b) Multiple entry and exit options and a credit accumulation system
Explanation: The concepts of multiple entry/exit and the Academic Bank of Credit (ABC) are central to promoting lifelong learning. They allow individuals to learn, earn credits, take a break to work, and then return to education to upskill or reskill at any point in their lives.
87. Question: The NEP 2020's focus on skill development is aligned with India's need for:
a) A workforce with only theoretical knowledge
b) A workforce equipped with 21st-century skills
c) A reduction in the number of skilled workers
d) A workforce trained only for government jobs
Answer: b) A workforce equipped with 21st-century skills
Explanation: The policy recognizes the changing demands of the global economy. It aims to create a workforce that has not just domain-specific knowledge but also critical 21st-century skills like critical thinking, problem-solving, creativity, and collaboration.
88. Question: What is the role of the National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF) in NEP 2020?
a) To replace the higher education framework
b) To provide a common framework for linking vocational qualifications and academic qualifications
c) To regulate private skill training providers
d) To conduct skill-based examinations
Answer: b) To provide a common framework for linking vocational qualifications and academic qualifications
Explanation: The NSQF is a competency-based framework that organizes all qualifications according to a series of levels of knowledge, skills, and aptitude. NEP 2020 aims to integrate NSQF seamlessly with the National Higher Education Qualification Framework (NHEQF) to create a unified system.
89. Question: The policy's approach to vocational education is to:
a) Keep it completely separate from academic streams
b) 'Blur the lines' between vocational and academic education
c) Make it mandatory for all students to choose a vocation
d) Focus only on traditional crafts
Answer: b) 'Blur the lines' between vocational and academic education
Explanation: The vision is to create a holistic and integrated system where students can easily move between vocational and academic streams. For example, a student in a vocational program can pursue higher academic studies, and a student in an academic stream can acquire vocational skills.
90. Question: The policy encourages collaboration between schools and:
a) Only government bodies
b) Local artisans, craftsmen, and businesses
c) International organizations only
d) Only other schools
Answer: b) Local artisans, craftsmen, and businesses
Explanation: To make vocational education relevant and practical, the policy encourages schools to partner with local experts, artisans, and businesses. This will provide students with authentic learning experiences and help preserve local crafts and skills.
91. Question: What is the ultimate goal of integrating vocational education into the school curriculum?
a) To prepare every student for a specific job
b) To respect and celebrate diversity in skills and talents
c) To reduce the importance of academic subjects
d) To make school education more expensive
Answer: b) To respect and celebrate diversity in skills and talents
Explanation: The policy aims to create a society where all forms of skills and talents are valued equally. By integrating vocational education, it seeks to give students the opportunity to discover and develop their unique talents, whether they are academic, artistic, or vocational.
92. Question: The 'Skill Labs' proposed in schools will be used for:
a) Computer-based learning only
b) Hands-on, practical training in various vocational skills
c) Conducting examinations
d) Storing school equipment
Answer: b) Hands-on, practical training in various vocational skills
Explanation: To facilitate vocational training, the policy suggests setting up 'Skill Labs' in schools. These labs will be equipped with the necessary tools and resources for students to get hands-on experience in different vocational areas.
93. Question: The policy's emphasis on vocational education is also aimed at:
a) Increasing the unemployment rate
b) Promoting entrepreneurship
c) Reducing the number of students going to college
d) Making education more theoretical
Answer: b) Promoting entrepreneurship
Explanation: By providing students with practical skills and an understanding of the world of work from an early age, the policy aims to foster an entrepreneurial mindset. Students will be better equipped to start their own ventures or become self-employed.
