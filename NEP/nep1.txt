1. The National Education Policy 2020 was approved by the Union Cabinet of India in which month and year?
A) January 2020
B) July 2020
C) November 2020
D) March 2021
Answer: B) July 2020
Explanation: The Union Cabinet approved the National Education Policy 2020 on 29th July 2020, replacing the 34-year-old National Policy on Education (NPE) of 1986. 
2. The new 5+3+3+4 curricular structure of school education corresponds to which age groups?
A) 3-18 years
B) 4-19 years
C) 5-17 years
D) 6-18 years
Answer: A) 3-18 years
Explanation: The new structure aims to universalize education from Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) to Grade 12, covering the developmental age group of 3-18 years. 
3. The first stage of the new school structure, "Foundational Stage," covers which ages?
A) 3-6 years
B) 3-8 years
C) 4-7 years
D) 5-8 years
Answer: B) 3-8 years
Explanation: The Foundational Stage is divided into two parts: 3 years of preschool or Anganwadi (ages 3-6) and 2 years of primary school (Grades 1-2, ages 6-8). 
4. The "Preparatory Stage" in the new school structure covers which grades?
A) Grades 1-2
B) Grades 3-5
C) Grades 6-8
D) Grades 9-10
Answer: B) Grades 3-5
Explanation: The Preparatory Stage covers ages 8-11, corresponding to Grades 3, 4, and 5. It focuses on play, discovery, and activity-based learning. 
5. The "Middle Stage" in the new school structure covers which grades?
A) Grades 3-5
B) Grades 4-6
C) Grades 6-8
D) Grades 9-11
Answer: C) Grades 6-8
Explanation: The Middle Stage covers ages 11-14, corresponding to Grades 6, 7, and 8. The focus shifts to more abstract concepts and subject teaching. 
6. The "Secondary Stage" in the new school structure covers which grades?
A) Grades 8-10
B) Grades 9-10
C) Grades 9-12
D) Grades 10-12
Answer: C) Grades 9-12
Explanation: The Secondary Stage covers ages 14-18, corresponding to Grades 9, 10, 11, and 12. It includes multidisciplinary study and greater critical thinking. 
7. NEP 2020 aims to achieve 100% Gross Enrollment Ratio (GER) in school education by which year?
A) 2025
B) 2030
C) 2035
D) 2040
Answer: B) 2030
Explanation: A key target of the policy is to bring all children in the age group of 3-18 years into the school system by 2030, achieving 100% GER. 
8. What is the primary aim of the "National Mission on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy"?
A) To promote higher education
B) To ensure that every child attains foundational literacy and numeracy by Grade 3
C) To digitalize all schools
D) To increase teacher salaries
Answer: B) To ensure that every child attains foundational literacy and numeracy by Grade 3
Explanation: This mission is an urgent national priority, aiming to ensure that by 2025, every student in Grade 3 achieves foundational skills in reading, writing, and arithmetic. 
9. According to NEP 2020, what should be the primary medium of instruction until at least Grade 5?
A) English
B) Hindi
C) Mother tongue or local language
D) Sanskrit
Answer: C) Mother tongue or local language
Explanation: The policy strongly advocates for the use of the mother tongue or local language as the medium of instruction to improve foundational learning, preferably until Grade 8 and beyond. 
10. The policy emphasizes that the three-language formula will need to be implemented in its spirit, promoting:
A) Monolingualism
B) Bilingualism
C) Multilingualism
D) Classical languages only
Answer: C) Multilingualism
Explanation: The spirit of the new three-language formula is to promote multilingual competence among students, helping them to appreciate the diversity of India. 
11. Which of the following is NOT a guiding principle of NEP 2020?
A) Recognizing, identifying, and fostering the unique capabilities of each student
B) Flexibility in the choice of learning pathways
C) Emphasis on rote learning and high-stakes board exams

Answer: C) Emphasis on rote learning and high-stakes board exams
Explanation: NEP 2020 explicitly aims to move away from rote learning and reduce the stress of high-stakes board examinations, focusing instead on conceptual understanding. 
12. NEP 2020 proposes to increase the public investment in the education sector to what percentage of GDP?
A) 4%
B) 5%
C) 6%
D) 7%
Answer: C) 6%
Explanation: The policy sets a target of reaching 6% of GDP for public investment in education, which is seen as crucial for achieving its goals.

A) Vocational education
B) Coding
C) A foreign language
D) All of the above
Answer: D) All of the above
Explanation: NEP 2020 mandates that every student will have access to vocational education, coding, and a foreign language as an elective during the middle stage.

A) The study of children
B) The theory and practice of education
C) The art of storytelling
D) The science of psychology
Answer: B) The theory and practice of education
Explanation: Pedagogy encompasses the strategies, methods, and activities of instruction. It is the discipline concerned with the theory and practice of teaching.

Explanation: The policy calls for curriculum content reduction to make space for holistic development, critical thinking, and deeper understanding.

B) NAC
C) HEGC

Explanation: The General Education Council (GEC) will frame the National Higher Education Qualification Framework (NHEQF) and specify learning outcomes for higher education.

Answer: C) 50%
Explanation: The policy sets an ambitious target to increase the GER in higher education, including vocational education, to 50% by 2035.

Explanation: The ABC will digitally store the academic credits earned by a student from various institutions, allowing for flexible, multidisciplinary learning pathways.

Explanation: The idea is to grant more autonomy to good-performing institutions while having a strong, transparent system to check substandard quality, corruption, and malpractices.

Explanation: The National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE), under the new HECI framework, will be restructured and strengthened as the premier body for teacher education.
